Expanding Support for Children + Families

Creating better outcomes for children living in poverty requires both providing direct support to families and championing solutions with greater reach. Tipping Point grantee Kidango is leveraging on-the-ground expertise to create statewide impact. In addition to providing programs and services to children and parents, Kidango sponsored a state bill (SB1047) that expanded access to early learning and care programs for infants and toddlers, and makes these services more easily accessible to the families that need it the most.

A Path To + Through College

A Tipping Point grantee since 2014, College Track supports students that are the first in their families to attend college—and provides them with the tools to persist both in higher education and on the pathway to a career. In the past year, 99% of program participants enrolled in college. College Track’s “best fit approach” helps students select universities committed to supporting first-generation and low-income students, ultimately ensuring higher graduation rates and laying the foundation for a prosperous future. Alumni aged 30 and over who have secured full-time jobs have an average annual income of over $90,000 ($20,000 more than the national median).

Ensuring Career Jobs Are Within Reach

A stable job is a critical foothold on the path towards economic security.  Tipping Point grantees like JVS provide the support and training needed to assist on that journey. 80% of graduates of JVS’s skills-training programs land a job paying an average of $30 an hour, and are on track to increase their earnings by 91% on average within two years. For Jamice Jones, a graduate of JVS’s automotive training program and the first female apprentice automotive machinist employed by the city of San Francisco, this investment means she can afford to stay in her hometown and raise her two children in safety and financial security. Hear Jamice’s story:

“For the first time, it feels like someone is on my side. A life full of abundance can happen for people like me who grew up in poverty—our refrigerators full, our kids happy.”
-Jamice Jones

Bold Solutions to Create Supportive Housing Solutions

Three years ago, Tipping Point pursued a new way of building housing for those in need faster and more affordably than ever before. Now, where a former parking lot once stood, the Tahanan Supportive Housing community is home to 145 residents who were previously unhoused. Projects like Tahanan show us that when we invest in and pursue new ideas and approaches, solutions to our community’s most pressing issues can be found.

“I’m almost 58 years old, and this is the first place I’ve lived in that’s mine. That’s my home.”
– Timothy Isaiah, Tahanan Supportive Housing Resident

Investing in Organizations Led by People of Color

With the right resources and support, organizations led by people with proximate experiences to the clients they serve can create greater impact. Tipping Point’s inaugural POC-Led Fellowship gathered nine leaders of color from across our grantee portfolio for a collaboratively-designed program, providing them with professional development opportunities, peer-to-peer networking, and access to more funding opportunities. Providing grantees with the support they need is changing the philanthropy landscape and making economic mobility possible for the people who need it most. Our impact will continue to grow with our second cohort launching in April 2023.

Being a Tipping Point grantee has opened doors to donors and advisors it would have taken me years to reach on my own. Their investment in me as a leader of color has helped me unapologetically step out on behalf of the young people Braven serves.

Diana Phuong, Executive Director, Braven Tipping Point grantee

Investing in Organizations Led by People of Color

On any given night, 3,000 young people are experiencing homelessness in the Bay Area. Progress is possible when we give people the right support at the right time. Tipping Point’s latest initiative, made possible by Visa Foundation, is a three-year, $16M effort targeting youth and young adults, ages 18 to 24, who are currently experiencing homelessness or at risk of homelessness.  By supporting and coordinating organizations on the ground, advocating for policy change, and providing seed funding for new approaches, Tipping Point can champion holistic approaches to more effectively put resources where they can have the most impact.

Being a Tipping Point grantee has opened doors to donors and advisors it would have taken me years to reach on my own. Their investment in me as a leader of color has helped me unapologetically step out on behalf of the young people Braven serves.

Ali Sutton Tipping Point Chief Program Officer
